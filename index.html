How Pet Insurance Works

It's simple: you pay the vet at the time of service, then submit a claim to your insurance provider. Once approved, you get reimbursed for covered expenses—typically 70-90% of the bill.

  1. Visit any vet — No network restrictions
  2. Pay the bill — At the time of service
  3. Submit claim — Online or via mobile app
  4. Get reimbursed — Direct deposit or check

Frequently Asked Questions

What is pet insurance and how does it work? +

Pet insurance helps cover unexpected veterinary costs. You pay monthly premiums, visit any licensed vet when your pet needs care, pay the bill, then submit a claim to get reimbursed — typically 70–90% of covered costs.

Is Paws & Tails an insurance company? +

No — we are a licensed independent insurance agency in California. We represent multiple top-rated carriers and help you find the best plan for your pet and budget. We are not an insurer ourselves.

When should I enroll my pet? +

The sooner, the better! Most carriers have waiting periods (10–14 days for illness, 2–5 days for accidents), and pre-existing conditions are typically excluded. Enrolling your pet while they're young and healthy gives you the widest coverage.

Are there any breed restrictions? +

All three of our partner carriers cover all dog breeds and cat breeds, including purebreds and mixed breeds. Some hereditary conditions may have specific waiting periods.

Can I use any veterinarian? +

Yes! All three of our carrier partners allow you to visit any licensed veterinarian or specialist in the United States and Canada — no network restrictions.

How much does pet insurance cost? +

Plans typically start from $15–$30/month for dogs and $9–$20/month for cats, depending on breed, age, location, and coverage level. Contact us for a free personalized quote.

What is not covered by pet insurance? +

Pre-existing conditions, elective procedures, and cosmetic treatments are generally not covered. Routine/preventive care may require an optional wellness add-on.

How do I file a claim? +

Each carrier has an online portal or mobile app for claim submission. Simply upload your vet invoice and medical records. Claims are typically processed within 2–5 business days.
